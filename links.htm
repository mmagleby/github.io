Links


This is by no means an exhaustive list of EAW sites.  For that, you should visit Sandbagger's EAW Links, which includes a descriptive list of EAW sites as well as a catalog of add-ons and updates on new releases.  The following are just a few sites that are good starting points for exploring the world of EAW.


Sandbagger's EAW Links - The comprehensive catalog of all EAW sites.  Includes listings by add-ons and is updated very frequently.  Indispensable!  Also check out Sandbagger's excellent new EAW guide "The Starting Gate"

Cord's EAW Page - This is THE site for EAW.  A great place to go to browse around and if you're looking for something, chances are its available from Cord's site.  

SimHQ.com - The EAW forum here is the gathering place for many of the EAW community.  Check this out for news of new add-ons, to ask a question, or just talk about anything EAW.  A Very friendly group, and checking the forum often will become an addiction.  Search the archive for questions you may have that have already been answered.

Mosi's Flightsim Page  - Don't miss this site, Mosi has been a pioneer in EAW skins and you will no doubt be looking at some of the best around. 

Serb's EAW flightline - Wonderful site with a lot of great content.  Serb has some especially good sounds and Me-262s!

Aces Rising - Some very fine skins and a beautiful site.  If you like Bf-109Es and Fw-190As (and who doesn't) you will be astounded with the number and quality of Julio's masterpieces.

Migge's EAW Pages - Migge has been around a long time with a ton of beautiful skins and cockpits.

-E's European Air War Skins 'n Stuff - Great all-around site that has been around a long time.  Check out the speech packs and the nice RAF birds.

Flying Sheep's Flugwerke - Some very nice skins of many different planes.

The Hanger - Otherwise known as Airbuddah's site, it dates back to the early days of EAW.  This has a very large collection of skins from various artists.  

Emil's EAW Campaigns - Don't miss Emil's masterful campaigns for EAW, including an updated BoB, Normandy, Ardennes, and Malta campaigns.  They add a completely new dimension to the game. 

Charles' EAW Site - Creator of the famous ECAPanel, the ground-breaking add-on that opened up EAW to use virtually any model of plane imaginable.  Charles' EAW Editing Notes are required reading if you want to learn about the inner workings of EAW.

VonOben's Flight Sim Mods - VonOben has some nice terrains and other useful mods.  He also has an excellent EAW tutorial.

Combatsim.com - Another good EAW forum, but this became a pay site and many people moved over to SimHQ.  But if you have membership, the archived forum is a great place to search to find info back to the beginnings of EAW.

Favorite Flight Sim Stuff - A very nicely done site by a true fan of EAW.  Check out the Black Box Interviews for a very interesting read about some of the mod-makers and other people behind EAW.

EAW Survey - A short survey about EAW and the EAW community.  Very interesting results.  Long live EAW!
